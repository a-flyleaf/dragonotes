If you've somehow stumbled upon this page without knowing what it is, hi! [Flight Rising](https://www1.flightrising.com/) is a browser-based dragon collection game, on which many people write little stories and develop worlds for their dragons. FR has an established setting, Sornieth, but players are free to pick and choose which aspects of the "canonical" lore they use (if any). **Subspecies** are player-defined dragon varieties, typically centered around a distinct aesthetic and common lore. Although the subspecies creator sets some benchmarks, individual dragons' stories are up to the people who own them. I think it's a neat semi-collaborative schtick, and decided to turn a long-standing personal lore idea into one. I do not like maintaining lengthy BBCode-heavy forum threads. Early marks matched genes categorized formally as Bar, Daub, Ripple, and Current, in various combinations. Other muted marks have also been documented, including but not limited to spots, stripes, and patches. Accent colors are sparse, when existent at all. Distinct, "artificial" patterns, such as those exhibited by Harlequin/Jester, are nigh-nonexistent; they tend to provide poor camoflauge. Although the environmental advantage of the marking has been lost to geographical spread, Frostbite dragons often exhibit **pale, "frosty" cracks** (Ice tertiary) across their entire body. Moreso than any base texture, this characteristic crackling serves as an in-group signifier---especially in territories far removed from the Icefield.
