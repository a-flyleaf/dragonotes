## subspecies brainstorming
- colors: **Midnight/Obsidian/Ice**. Other monochromes or adjacent are acceptable, but a **blue tint** is preferred. Possible brown-tinted variant?
	- <small>(lowkey based off my OG dragons with this combo: [Aeron](https://www1.flightrising.com/dragon/14577448) (plus kids), [Frostbite](https://www1.flightrising.com/dragon/8030460) (lol), and [Hydra](https://www1.flightrising.com/dragon/23893039). That particular trio has lore crumbs [here [the Flight&nbsp;Reaping tag]](https://www1.flightrising.com/forums/link?url=https%3A%2F%2Farchaeoraptor.tumblr.com%2Ftagged%2FFlight%2520Reaping%2Fchrono); all that’s really relevant now is that they had a Frostbite offshoot and got Reapening’d. Gone to the shadows. Irrelevant now. Anyway,)</small>
- genes: **monochrome** primary+secondary, **Crackle** tertiary. Similarly-busy terts are acceptable; it’s about the Coverage. Ideal terts affect both body and wings (so Rift wouldn’t work, Wish would), and I’m veering towards *no linebreakers*. But maybe those can get their own little variant lore.
	- Emphasis on **natural patterns**, animalistic. Harlequin/Jester is out, Tiger/Stripes is in. Non-mammalian genes like Arapaima or Hawkmoth are borderline; Python/Morph is great bc they’re monochrome, Poison/Toxin… less so.
- element: **Ice** for the “true”… version? adherent? Definitely *originated* in Ice, have long since spread. Other elements get their own special flavor.
- breed: **All fair game!** \*See below for lore implications.

For some qualifying scries, I cooked up a bunch of Ancient variants in [Altair’s bio](https://www1.flightrising.com/dragon/13424371). Some are… not my favorite, but they get the point across.

- Dragons do **not** have to be related to $insertFoundingPairHere. This would be an open thing, not a lineage; gene a dragon to fit the requirements, congrats they count. Unrelated dragons joining the ~effort might be encouraged?
- The core colors & genes are definitely **old-style** (monochrome, Crackle, old color wheel; preceded the concept of an Ancient breed). Lean into that? Probably **not Shade-related** just because that lore doesn’t interest me too much, but involving Gaolers isn’t off the table because I think they’re cool. (Pun not intended.)
- Possible emphasis on **exaltation**? Keeping that OG Frostbite throughline: servitude to the deities is of utmost priority. Dragons *do not* have to be level 25 to qualify, let alone fully-statted/-stoned, but in these hypothetical $subspecies societies\*, they get the highest honors. Best thing a dragon can do is get yeeted; second-best, be the one training others for the yeetening.
- \*It’s like how official FR lore tends towards mono-species lairs, when in reality monolairs are the exception, not the norm. This subspecise tends towards a **unity of purpose**, with color & marks being a practical consideration. **Nocturnal ambushers?** Which ig makes Tundras the prototypical breed (see: [Ambush](https://www1.flightrising.com/game-database/item/687)), but really, they don’t discriminate.
- Initially based in the **southeastern Southern Icefields**; past the Cloudscape Crags, more into the Reclaimer’s Glacier, Dripcave Dregs, and Fortress of Ends. Maybe the actual central base is/was the Glacier, where the dark base colors with icy streaks made for perfect camouflage. Also seen among rocky shorelines?
- Hmm, Gaoler lore tie-in: started as a small band of Tundras who dragged suspected… okay fine *maybe* Shade-touched, but the powerful guys were best left to the big guys (Gaolers themselves). *These* fuzzbutts operated much more insidiously: spy on & later integrate themselves with other clans, *then* nab dragons thought to be colluding with Evil Dark Forces. **Social hysteria**, basically. Crusaders of presumed moral(?) contagion. The Shade was only ever a scapegoat.

## subspecies brainstorming, lore ed. (continued)
- At first, scouts (Seekers? based on [Gaoler lore](https://www1.flightrising.com/wiki/wiki/article/gaoler-dragons), but with an adjacent-albeit-synonymous name) were expected to report back to base with their catches and findings. This became increasingly impractical as the group expanded. More important to get the job done, as quickly and far-reaching as possible, than travel all the way back up the chain of command to jail one (1) suspect.
- Thus developed something of a **hivemind mentality**, a natural extension of the existing purpose-first utilitarian ideals. Dragons seeking to take charge and hold power were shunned; The Cause™ ought to precede them. (This can and has been abused, of course. As any mentality can be.)
- skimming the [Tundra lore](https://www1.flightrising.com/wiki/wiki/article/tundra-dragons): The breed’s inclinations towards short-term individualistic memory + long-held grudges + avoiding outright battle all, I think, feed in nicely to what’s already been established. What use, for instance, is becoming a monarch when your peers only recall you as friend or foe? They’ll remember the claws you stepped on in your climb. Relatedly, what better dragons to keep a running tally of others’ Crimes and Sins, the impression of blame lasting long after the initial offense is forgotten?

(wait. Wait. [purification in progress](https://offtherpg.com/).... purity of Moral of Behavior of Purpose. appearance less important, bloodline *wholly* unimportant, we are not doing dragon discrimination here.)

- The only tenet lost is the founding group’s **ambush technique**, subsumed over generations by an **all-purpose training regiment**. Developed tactics still **prioritize efficiency** (read: glass cannon fodder trainers), but the details vary by clan. Taking a page from Plague’s playbook, it is **more important to thrive and proliferate** than to adhere to some dated, prescriptive norm.

<br>
And now for some specificity: *how* the tenets are *expressed* in each flight! Or at least Ice, Plague, and Lightning, since. y’know, authorial bias.

- **Ice** $subspecies clans are primarily clustered in the **Reclaimer’s Glacier**, a formidable force in plain sight. It’s rumored that ~~captives~~ dragons “saved” by these clans may, if deemed dangerous enough, be sent directly down the Dripcave Dregs for further… study? containment? Or perhaps a *reinvigoration*, emerging years or even decades later as something better. Improved. More imposing than any dragon who hasn’t passed the Gaoler’s gates. Perhaps a trip straight to the Icewarden would have been a mercy....  
&emsp;Internally, these clans are often too ephemeral to establish distinct identities. Relative to other $subspecies outposts, they remain closest to the original ideals, with only Tundras and Gaolers able to subsist in the cold generations down.
- As dictated by the landforms, the Starfall Isles are one of the first territories that $subspecies expanded into. Here they made a strong case for continuing to proliferate outwards, with access to new forms of magic and the cooperation of less-frigidly-inclined breeds (more populous here than the Icefields) unlocking strength and power that far surpassed that of any snow-brain.  
&emsp;Oh, yeah. They developed bias out here. That’s a thing that happened, between the integration of new dragons into the ranks & the very establishment of an outgroup (relative to the originals) encouraging formation of a distinct identity. Heck, maybe $subspecies wasn’t even named in their home territory; all the terminology, classifications, Established Ways of Being: they got defined out here, among scholars and scientists.  
&emsp;**Arcane** $subspecies clans enjoy punting their trainees to the stars and calculating the trajectories. Idk, this got kind of out of hand for a spontaneous addition.
- Traveling eastward, $subspecies clans of the **Plague** inclination really take that “survival of the fittest” misconception and go *our city now*. “But isn’t Plague about evolution and adaptation and not social darwinism” WRONG not as $subspecies took it. Hatchlings raised in the Scarred Wasteland are often subject to all sorts of viral concoctions as a highly unethical vaccination-through-exposure speedrun wringer. Those who survive, if not sent to the Wyrmwound, are often toughened up to help train the next generation of survivors.  
&emsp;[Galena](https://www1.flightrising.com/dragon/20722709) was nabbed by and raised into one such offshoot. Hated it. Spent a long time avoiding it like the, uh, plague. Only with the companionship of [an old trekker](https://www1.flightrising.com/dragon/397435) has he started circling his origins again. (The “official” subspecies writeup(s) might be in their voice(s). Based on their research? As a treat.)
- In **Shadow**, you won’t know about $subspecies clans until it’s too late. Maybe they hosted that big Flight Reaping nonsense. Entertainer bend (among themselves, of course), complete with gladiatorial matches in the Forum of the Obscured Crescent.
- [**Lightning**:] My “fodder factory” tab does not exist in lore (yet?) but these utilitarian losers run those sorts of shows over here. 24/7 grindhouse nightmare clans. They don’t even have CEOs. Who needs regulations, though; they tend to hide out among the Carrion Canyon and other isolated patches, where ethical conventions can’t touch them. Get back to work! *Be more efficient!*

aaand quickfire thoughts on the remaining flights:
- **Wind**: Groups from Plague ventured in all directions in no particular order. Probably did not see much success down here; too cheery, everyone’s smiling and flying kites and wigs them out :(  
&emsp;Clans that *did* make posts among the winds like to settle around the Twisting Crescendo like storm-chasing masochists. Just throw the fodder directly into the swirling vortex just heckign Fling Them,
- **Water**: Some poor Guardian(s) got dragged under and went *you know what would be great down here? Go Deeper.* Deeper than Ice’s Dregs, so deep not a speck of light filters through. In recent years there’s been no Tidelord for them to serve and they’re kinda bummed out about that. Maybe they’re self-cannibalizing.  
&emsp;Guardian- and Undertide-heavy, for obvious reasons. So secretive that, like the Shadow inhabitants, they’ve kinda started to evolve into their own unique, disparate Thing.
- **Fire** is probably sparsely-populated, on account of the unions. The geographical reach is also bad. C’mon, these guys originated in Ice; maybe some weirdos saw it as a ~test of power~ to establish themselves here, but mostly it’s dismissed as a waste of time and effort. (Sorry, Fire. I like you guys plenty. Alas, the lore does not mesh.)
	- <small>(great, now I’m thinking Altair has some story related to being mistaken for a $subspecies dragon… hmm.)</small>
- **Light**: Luminax worship was my first thought, but. Y’know what’d be more fun? Luminax *competitors*. Definitely kind of abnormal about Imperials, as a rule, what with the whole Emperor-zombie thing. They traveled all the way over here, heard about that, went :O and suddenly Lightweaver is their #1 deity of all time.  
&emsp;They camp out in the Hewn City like a bunch of edgelords. The Lightning offshoots came from this direction, after some groups took too much of the scholarly sauce and wanted to establish themselves somewhere less, y’know, Emperor-ravaged and Shadow-secretive.
- **Earth**: North of Plague, these groups probably saw the *best success* of all cardinal directions. (Small homage to Earth being the dom powerhouse <i lang="fr">du jour</i> for several years and counting, yes.) The kind of clans that, like the rest of the Flight, either fit like a glove or see themselves out, with little in-between. Have possibly probably largely assimilated into greater Earth culture by now.
- **Nature**, likewise, is not so much home to $subspecies groups as it is a place where some dragons originating *from* $subspecies clans happen to live now. Some traveled east from Dragonhome; others fled northeast from the Tangled Wood or Sunbeam Ruins. The latter somehow survived the water-crossing, and are subsequently more likely to have aquatic inclinations.

…You know, ~realistically, they definitely stop adhering to the Ice tert after leaving the territory where that actually, feasibly provides camouflage. Gameplay-lore clash, ig; like, they *could* expand to other Crackle colorations, but at that point they’re different subspecies entirely. Lines are ever blurred with these sorts of things.

Basically, further geographical stray = further societal (and, theoretically, aesthetic) divide from the origin. At a certain point you’re just looking at a $deity-obsessed clan. On *paper*, the anti-individualist tendencies dissuade these guys from establishing firm identities in other Flights—but also, opposition to something (say, the more individual or communal lifestyles more common elsewhere) can be a foundation in its own right. Idk, needs more thought.

If this idea lingers I should stick it on my site :Vc If nothing else, I like the idea of retconning all my vague and formless lore-antagonist-groups into being These Guys. More of a personal NPC playground that others can join in on if they want. But for now I’m off to do not-lore things.
